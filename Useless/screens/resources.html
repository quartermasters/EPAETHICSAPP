Frequently Asked Questions

What are the ethics rules for federal employees?

Federal employees are subject to a comprehensive set of ethics rules designed to ensure integrity and impartiality in government service. These rules cover conflicts of interest, gifts, outside employment, and political activities to maintain public trust.

How do I report a potential ethics violation?

Report potential ethics violations to your supervisor, ethics official, or through the agency's designated reporting channels. You can also contact the Office of Inspector General for serious violations.

Where can I find more information on ethics training?

Additional ethics training resources are available through OGE.gov, your agency's ethics office, and specialized training programs. This app provides comprehensive modules covering all major ethics topics.

What should I do if I'm offered a gift?

Generally, you should politely decline gifts from prohibited sources. Some gifts under $20 may be acceptable, but always consult your ethics official if you're unsure about the rules.

Ethics Glossary

Conflict of Interest

A situation where personal interests could improperly influence official duties.

Prohibited Source

A person or organization that seeks official action from your agency or does business with your agency.

Financial Disclosure

Reports required to identify potential conflicts of interest based on financial holdings.
